Andrew Hellinger

My mission is to increase the safety and equity of our transportation systems using emerging technologies like autonomous vehicles, ridesharing, and e-scooters. I recognize that motor vehicles are currently the most dangerous form of transportation and am energized by the tremendous safety improvements that autonomous vehicles may bring. I see inequity in access to transportation across cities in the U.S. and am excited to be evaluating the use of e-scooters to address these inequities.

I graduated from Johns Hopkins University in May 2020 with a degree in public health and computer science, and I am currently researching transportation safety and equity at the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health. I have a broad and varied skillset with strong experience in design, software engineering, communication, and writing.
